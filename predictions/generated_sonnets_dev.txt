Thou blind fool, Love, what dost thou to mine eyes
That they behold and see not what they see?
They know what beauty is, see where it lies, in their own hearts.
But here is another beauty, tell, that is but yet out of sight.
The sole waste of mine mind, still well deceived,
Let them look, not only at beauty but in beauty's presence;
For my sons are my daughters, saying in me love thus;
Therefore at once they look upon me and see me,
And while they see I do not write back,
And I I find no eye in mine eye,
To live then my beauty or die with my death;
  The wind shines, but war's fire interferes.
